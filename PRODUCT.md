# PRODUCT.md

## Users
Senior engineering hiring managers, tech leads, and recruiters at remote-first companies globally. They evaluate portfolios in 2-5 minutes, on laptop or desktop, during work hours. They've seen hundreds of portfolios; they're looking for reasons to shortlist or dismiss.

## Brand
Eduard Pichardo Rochet. Full-stack software engineer, 4+ years experience, Dominican Republic, open to remote roles worldwide.

### Brand voice
Precise. Calm. Substantive. Communicates seniority through restraint and evidence, not flash. Every claim backed by a metric. No hype, no buzzwords without substance.

### Register
Brand. Portfolio = design IS the product. A hiring manager's impression is the thing being made.

### Anti-references
- Generic dev portfolios (dark + neon cyan, dot grids, card grids with emoji icons)
- AI-generated landing pages (Fraunces + DM Mono + cream, editorial-magazine aesthetic)
- SaaS templates (hero-metric-big-number, identical card grids)
- Junior developer portfolios (too many badges, too much color, no whitespace)

### Strategic principles
1. Quality over quantity. Fewer projects, deeper presentation.
2. Evidence over claims. Metrics, screenshots, architecture decisions.
3. Substance over style. Design serves the content; content proves competence.
4. Restraint signals confidence. Senior engineers don't need to shout.

## Tone
Professional but warm. Not corporate, not casual. The tone of a senior engineer explaining their work to a respected peer.

## Key differentiators
- Cross-stack fluency (TS/JS, Java, C#, Kotlin)
- Regulated industry experience (fintech, telecom)
- AI-augmented development workflow
- Full-stack + mobile delivery
- Bilingual (Spanish native, English professional)
